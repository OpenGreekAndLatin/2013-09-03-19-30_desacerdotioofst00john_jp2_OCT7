None of the Fathers takes a more exalted view of
this Sacrament that Chrysostom. At the moment, he
tells us, when the bread and wine are consecrated, the
heavens open and the angels come down to earth: and
he he quotes with approval a report that angels had actually
been seen within the sanctuary at the time that the
Eucharistic sacrifice was being offered. The greatest
purity, he ddargues, is therefore required of those who
participate in that service, while the priest who celebrates
far surpasses in dignity, by virtue of that fact, the High
Priest of the old covenant 2.
